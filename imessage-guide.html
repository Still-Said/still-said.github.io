iMessage Export Guide

Get your messages ready

Export your iPhone text messages in four steps so we can turn them into a keepsake. Takes about five minutes. Requires a Mac.

Windows PC? Email us at stillsaid@proton.me and we'll help.

Step one

Download the export tool

Click the button below to download a small file. It does everything automatically.

Download export tool

Find the downloaded file in your Downloads folder. It's called stillsaid-export.command.

Double-click it to run. A black Terminal window will open — that's expected.

If your Mac says it can't be opened: Right-click the file, choose "Open" from the menu, then click "Open" again in the dialog that appears. This only happens the first time.
Step two

Give permission to read your messages

The first time you run the tool, it will tell you that Terminal needs permission to access your messages. This is a standard macOS security feature. Here's how to turn it on:

1. Open System Settings from the Apple menu at the top-left of your screen.

2. Click Privacy & Security in the left sidebar.

3. Scroll down the list on the right and click Full Disk Access.

5. Your Mac will ask to quit Terminal. Click "Terminate".

6. Reopen Terminal, then double-click the export file again to re-run it.

Why is this needed? Your messages are stored in a protected file on your Mac. This permission lets the tool read that file. Nothing is sent anywhere — it all stays on your computer. You can turn this off again after you're done.
Step three

The export runs automatically

Once permission is granted, the tool installs what it needs and exports your messages. You don't need to do anything — just wait for it to finish. It usually takes a minute or two.

When it's done, a folder called StillSaid Export will appear on your Desktop and open automatically.

What's in this folder? A separate text file for every conversation on your phone — one file per person or group chat, named by phone number or contact name. The folder contains all your conversations, but you only send us the one you want.
Step four

Pick your conversation and send it to us

Open the StillSaid Export folder on your Desktop. Browse the files and find the conversation you'd like turned into a keepsake. Files are named by phone number or contact name — if you're not sure which is which, open a few to check.

Email just that one file to:

stillsaid@proton.me

We only see the conversation you choose to send. Everything else stays on your computer.

After you've sent the file, we recommend deleting the StillSaid Export folder from your Desktop. Your messages are in plain text in those files, so it's best not to leave them sitting around.
Frequently Asked Questions

How do I run the export tool I downloaded?

Find the file in your Downloads folder (it's called stillsaid-export.command) and double-click it. It will open a Terminal window and guide you through everything automatically. If your Mac says the file can't be opened because it's from an unidentified developer, right-click the file and choose "Open" instead.

The tool says Terminal needs Full Disk Access. What do I do?

Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Full Disk Access and toggle on Terminal. Your Mac will ask you to quit Terminal — click "Terminate," then reopen Terminal and double-click the export file again. This is a standard macOS security feature — the permission just lets the tool read your messages file. You can turn it off again after you're done.

Screenshot: Full Disk Access settings with Terminal toggled on

Why does Terminal need to restart after I change the permission?

macOS requires apps to restart before new permissions take effect. When you toggle on Full Disk Access for Terminal, your Mac will show a dialog asking to quit Terminal. Click "Terminate," then reopen Terminal. This only happens once.

The export folder has files for all my conversations. Do you see all of them?

No. The export creates a file for every conversation on your phone, but those files stay on your computer. We only see the specific file(s) you choose to email us. Everything else remains private.

How do I find the right conversation in the export folder?

Files are named by phone number (e.g., +15551234567.txt) or contact name. If you're not sure which is which, double-click a file to open it — you'll recognize the conversation from the messages inside. Look for the person whose messages you'd like turned into a keepsake.

Does this work on Windows?

This export tool requires a Mac. If you only have a Windows PC, email us at stillsaid@proton.me and we'll help you find another way to get your messages exported.

Will emojis and reactions show up in my keepsake?

Yes. The export preserves emojis, tapbacks (reactions like "Loved" or "Liked"), and all text formatting. They'll be part of your keepsake.

What about photos and videos?

The current export focuses on text messages. Photo and video support is something we're working on for future keepsakes.

Is this tool safe? Does it send my data anywhere?

The export tool is open-source software that anyone can inspect. It makes zero network connections — it reads your messages from a file on your Mac and writes text files to your Desktop. Nothing is uploaded, transmitted, or shared with anyone. The only time your data leaves your computer is when you choose to email us a file.

Should I delete the export folder when I'm done?

Yes, we recommend it. The exported files contain your messages in plain text, so it's best to delete the StillSaid Export folder from your Desktop after you've sent us the conversation you want.

My Mac says it can't verify the developer of this file.

This is normal for files downloaded from the internet. Right-click the file, choose "Open," and then click "Open" again in the dialog. macOS will remember your choice and won't ask again.
